# Nouri — Vision

**A personalized nutrition intelligence platform that helps growing kids hit their daily nutrition goals, build lasting healthy habits, and optimize their physical development — built for specific children, not generic demographics.**

*(Nouri)*

**Owner:** Chris Park
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-06

---

## Summary

Parents of growing kids have no reliable way to answer "are my kids eating the right things for *their* body, age, and activity level right now?" Generic nutrition apps are built for adults managing weight — they don't know your kid. Nouri is built specifically for Mason and Charlotte Park: it knows their growth stage, blood work, activity load, and food preferences, and becomes a smarter partner over time. Success is simple: the kids use it, they hit their pre-puberty growth window, and they build a relationship with nutrition that lasts the rest of their lives.

## 1. The Problems We're Solving

**Solve for this specific child before solving for children in general.**

---

### Problem 1: Parents Are Flying Blind at the Most Critical Growth Window

#### The pre-puberty window is irreversible — and there's no dashboard for it

**Current Reality:**
- Parents are making daily food decisions with zero nutritional visibility
- Generic apps assign demographic averages — a 12-year-old hockey player and a sedentary adult share the same protein target
- Blood work, doctor feedback, and growth data exist in isolation — never connected to what's on the plate
- "Just eat more" is advice, not a system

**The Impact:**
- Kids miss the pre-puberty growth window — the highest-leverage period for height, bone density, and athletic development
- Deficiencies accumulate silently: low vitamin D, insufficient calcium, not enough protein for the activity load
- Parents can't answer "is what we're doing actually working?" until it's too late to adjust

**Why This Matters:**
Pre-puberty is not recoverable. The bone density you build at 10–13 determines your adult skeleton. Missing this window isn't a setback — it's a permanent outcome. A parent who cares deeply about their child's growth deserves more than guesswork.

**Who this affects:** Parents of growing kids — especially those who are smaller, highly active, or have specific health signals from labs

---

### Problem 2: The Gap Between "What's Good for Them" and "What They'll Actually Eat"

#### Nutrition is only as good as what actually gets eaten

**Current Reality:**
- Each child has specific foods they love and foods they won't touch — meal prep defaults to the familiar
- Small optimizations (adding oil to pasta, calcium-rich sides) live in a parent's head and are never systematized
- No tool can suggest "new meals this specific kid will probably like, using ingredients that serve their growth"
- Two kids in the same household can have completely different needs and preferences

**The Impact:**
- Kids eat the same rotation of meals, missing nutritional variety
- Parents have no way to discover and vet new recipes against both nutrition goals AND per-child preferences
- Accumulated tips and tricks are never built upon

**Why This Matters:**
Optimizing for growth requires threading a needle — meals that are both strategically nutritious AND appealing to a specific 9 or 12-year-old. Generic apps don't know your kid. They can't thread that needle.

**Who this affects:** Parents planning and preparing meals for kids with distinct, established preferences

---

### Problem 3: Logging Has to Be Effortless or It Won't Happen

#### The whole system depends on data that's too hard to capture today

**Current Reality:**
- Existing trackers require precise portion weights, barcode scans, or manual USDA lookups
- The hardest part isn't naming the food — it's estimating how much was eaten
- If logging takes more than 30 seconds, it won't happen consistently
- Kids especially won't do it if there's friction

**The Impact:**
- Without consistent logging, nutritional visibility collapses
- Habits don't form because the behavior is too effortful to sustain
- The AI system is only as smart as the data it receives

**Why This Matters:**
The best nutrition intelligence in the world is useless without input. Logging is the foundation everything else is built on. It has to be fast enough for a kid to do themselves — a photo, a quick description — and smart enough to estimate portions from context clues.

**Who this affects:** Both kids as self-loggers; parents as meal loggers for the family

## 2. Strategic Positioning

### The Core Insight

**What everyone else builds:**
MyFitnessPal, Cronometer, Lose It — adult calorie trackers built for weight management. They use demographic averages (age/gender/height/weight) and treat everyone in a bucket identically.

**Why they don't work here:**
They don't know Mason. They don't know that he plays select hockey 5–6 times a week and is in a pre-puberty growth window where protein and calcium are particularly critical right now. They don't know what he'll eat or what his last blood panel showed. Generic apps optimize for the wrong outcome — weight management, not growth.

**The contrarian position:**
Nutrition apps assume nutrition is a universal math problem. It isn't — it's a deeply personal one. A tool that doesn't know the child it's feeding isn't much more useful than a Google search.

**The difference:**
- **Generic apps:** Demographic averages, weight management, built for adults
- **Nouri:** This specific child, growth optimization, personalized to biology + activity + preferences

Nouri is built for my kids first. Other families are welcome.

---

### The Three Strategic Pillars

#### 1. Know This Child

**The old way:** Enter age, gender, height, weight. Receive generic targets.
**The Nouri way:** Build a living profile — blood work, growth stage, activity schedule, food preferences, doctor feedback. Targets adapt as the child develops. The longer you use it, the smarter it gets.

- Blood work and lab results connect directly to nutrition targets
- Growth stage and activity level drive caloric and macro calculations
- Preferences are tracked and inform every suggestion

#### 2. Log Effortlessly

**The old way:** Weigh food, scan barcodes, select from dropdowns, estimate in cups.
**The Nouri way:** Describe it, photograph it, or say it. AI handles parsing, portion estimation, and nutrition lookup. Done in under 30 seconds.

- Natural language: *"Mason had 2 scrambled eggs, toast, and OJ"*
- Photo: snap the plate, AI estimates what's there
- Family meals: *"Both kids had tacos — Mason had 3, Charlotte had 2"*
- Quick re-log: one tap to repeat a recent meal

#### 3. Get Smarter Over Time

**The old way:** Static targets. No feedback loop. No learning.
**The Nouri way:** AI connects intake to outcomes, learns preferences, surfaces gaps, and makes proactive suggestions. Each week the system knows more.

- Flags nutritional gaps before they accumulate
- Learns what each kid will and won't eat — uses that to suggest new meals they're likely to try
- Proactive coaching: *"Mason's been low on calcium all week — try adding cheese to dinner tonight"*
- AI chat for anything: meal planning, lab result context, tournament-week nutrition

---

### What We're NOT Building

- ❌ A calorie counter for adults (we're building growth optimization for kids)
- ❌ A generic nutrition tracker (we're building a tool that knows this specific child)
- ❌ A recipe app (recipes are a reference library, not the core workflow)
- ❌ A medical device (nutritional guidance — "discuss with your doctor" where appropriate)
- ❌ A weight loss tool (the goal is growth optimization, not restriction)

## 3. Who This Is For

### Primary: The Park Household

Nouri is built for Chris and his wife, tracking Mason and Charlotte.

- **Chris & wife** — meal planners, primary loggers, the people making daily food decisions
- **Mason (12)** — select hockey 5–6x/week, pre-puberty, on the smaller side
- **Charlotte (9)** — gymnastics + dance, pre-puberty, on the smaller side

**Why they're underserved:**
- Generic apps use adult weight-management framing — wrong goal, wrong audience
- USDA tables are static and require manual interpretation
- Pediatric nutritionists are infrequent, expensive, and not connected to daily decisions

### Secondary: Parents of Growing Active Kids (App Store)

The problems Nouri solves are not unique to this household. Parents of growing kids — especially those in competitive youth sports or with specific lab results — face the same gaps. If other families find it on the App Store and use it, that's the secondary win.

**What they need:** A tool that knows their specific child, not generic demographic averages.

## 4. The Sequence

**Personal utility first → App Store second**

The right order is: prove it genuinely helps Mason and Charlotte, then publish for others to use. A tool that doesn't help the specific kids it was built for has no business going public.

---

### V1: Works for Our Family *(current)*

**Focus:** Make it genuinely useful for Mason and Charlotte day-to-day.

**Core capabilities:**
- Personalized profiles with activity-adjusted daily targets
- Effortless logging: text, photo, family meals, quick re-log
- Daily and weekly nutritional visibility with progress bars and trend charts
- AI-powered gap detection, per-meal tips, and proactive suggestions
- Ask Nouri conversational AI with full context on both kids
- Recipe library with per-child ratings

**V1 validates:** Can AI handle the heavy lifting well enough that logging stays under 30 seconds and insights are actually useful?

**V1 reality:** Core loop is working. Remaining backlog (B4–B8) completes the daily experience — day navigation, target transparency, meal history, ingredient constraints.

---

### V2: App Store Launch *(next)*

**Focus:** Publish Nouri so other families can use it and it exists as a live, showcaseable product.

**Core capabilities:**
- Multi-family onboarding (not just the Park household)
- iOS app or installable PWA on the home screen
- Self-serve profile creation for new families
- Hosted infrastructure stable enough for external users

**V2 goal:** Nouri is live on the App Store. Other families can find it and use it. The builder has a real shipped product to point to — not a private household tool, a public app. If families use it and love it, even better.

---

### V3: Smarter Over Time *(future)*

**Focus:** Deepen the intelligence based on what real usage reveals.

**Core capabilities:**
- Features driven by actual V1/V2 feedback — not speculation
- Deeper health integrations (wearables, pediatric growth curves, supplement tracking)
- Richer personalization as the system accumulates history per child

**V3 goal:** Nouri gets meaningfully smarter the longer a family uses it. The gap between "new user" and "1-year user" is obvious.
